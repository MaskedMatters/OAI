# OAI - Open Access Identifier

OAI is a free, dynamic alternative to DOI for researchers, scholars, and institutions. Currently, I am either too lazy or unprepared to right a full README.md file, so instead, a quick synopsis and To-Do list should prove sufficient.

If you navigate to the website OAI is being hosted, [https://oai.yaylabs.dev](https://oai.yaylabs.dev), you will be greeted with our nice landing page introducing our goal. It's a free and open-source replacement to the ISO standardised, DOI (Digital Object Identifier). It allows institutions to create persistent identifiers for their research, scholars, and other digital objects. The downside is that it requires going through a verified journal system and fees that make the entire process inaccessible to independent researchers and smaller institutions. OAI aims to fix this by providing a free and open-source alternative that is accessible to everyone and allows for individual users to apply for their own IDs.

Our service integrates with ORCiD to allow for seamless authentication and management of your OAI identifiers. The landing page of our website allows you to sign in with ORCiD, after which you will be redirected to our dashboard for easy viewing of the cool stufffff. The about us page shows off a bit of our nomenclature and how IDs get chosen as well which I think is interesting. When you login, the dashboard is pretty self-explanatory, you can create new IDs, edit existing ones, and delete them. If you have any questions, feel free to reach out to me through a GitHub issue or discussion.
